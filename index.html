I approach this work through the intersection of healthcare, technology, and power, focusing on how sociotechnical systems influence patient‚Äìprovider interactions and institutional responses to bias.

Research Frameworks

My work integrates multiple theoretical and methodological approaches to understand and address how power, identity, and technology intersect in healthcare settings.

Intersectionality

I use intersectionality as both an analytic and methodological intervention, examining how systems of privilege and oppression shape data practices, classification, and technological design decisions in healthcare informatics.

Feminist & Queer Theory

Drawing from feminist and queer theory, I interrogate how healthcare systems produce and sustain stigma, and how technologies can either reinforce or challenge cisheteronormative assumptions embedded in health information systems.

Human-Centered Design

I employ human-centered design and participatory research approaches that center patient experience while remaining attentive to system-level constraints and opportunities for change.

Community-Based Participatory Research

My methodological approach integrates qualitative interviews, inductive thematic analysis, and collaborative research practices that treat lived experience as empirical evidence and analytic input.

Featured Projects

My research projects center patient experiences of bias and discrimination to inform the design of more equitable healthcare technologies and practices.

UnBIASED Project

Lead Graduate Researcher | 2019‚Äì2024

A multi-year qualitative study examining how BIPOC and LGBTQ+ patients experience implicit bias in healthcare encounters. This project forms the empirical foundation of my dissertation research and has resulted in multiple peer-reviewed publications.

  • Led design and execution of 25+ in-depth patient interviews
  • Coordinated team-based qualitative coding and analysis workflows
  • Published findings in JAMIA (co-first author) and AMIA (first author)
  • Developed conceptual model of how bias breaks down patient-provider communication

Dissertation Research

PhD Candidate | In Progress

My dissertation extends the UnBIASED findings to explore how patient-identified sources of harm and patient-generated strategies for navigating biased healthcare systems can inform the design of technologies, workflows, and institutional practices that reduce stigma and improve care.

  • Categorizing patient strategies as expertise that surfaces where systems fail
  • Translating lived experiences into actionable guidance for healthcare improvement
  • Contributing to more equitable and accountable healthcare environments
